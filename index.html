Elephants

Mainly endangered due to deforestation of their habitat and to poaching to obtain the valuable ivory of thier tusks.

Panda

Their populations have declined due to habitat destruction as a result of the human population increase. Also, habitat destruction also leads to food shortages.

Tiger

The most immediate threat to wild tigers is poaching. Other factors include deforestation, climate change and human-tiger conflict

Giraffe

Aside from illegal poaching, giraffes are mainly threatened by habitat loss, civil unrest, and the adverse effects of climate change.

Orangutans

Mainly affected by the destruction and degradation of the tropical rain forest, high human activities and illegal animal traders

Polar Bear

Mainly threatened by ongoing and potential loss of their sea ice habitat resulting from climate change.

Jaguar

Deforestation poses the most threat to their existence along side human factors such as conflicts and illegal trading

Bonobo

The collective threats impacting wild bonobos include: poaching, civil unrest, habitat degradation, and a lack of information about the species.

Sea Turtle

Slaughtered for their eggs, meat, skin, and shells, they suffer from poaching and over-exploitation and also face habitat destruction

Rhino

Continously rising demand for rhino horn for remedies and decorations over the century threatens their existence
